The Journey

1969

Born in Helsinki, Finland — a city of engineers and long winters.

1981

Got his first computer at age 11 — a Commodore VIC-20. The obsession begins.

1988

Enrolled at the University of Helsinki — Computer Science. Fate was already written.

1991

Released Linux kernel v0.01 to the world. Just a hobby project, he said. />

1992

Linux became open source — unleashing a global movement of collaborative engineering.

1994

Linux kernel version 1.0 released — the OS was now production-grade and unstoppable.

2000

Linux adopted by major enterprises worldwide. IBM, Oracle, and the Fortune 500 take notice.

2005

Created Git — the distributed version control system now used by 100M+ developers globally.

2012

Named a Linux Foundation Fellow — an institution built around his life's work.

2015

Received the IEEE Computer Pioneer Award — recognized as a titan of modern computing.

2022

Linux turns 31 and powers 90% of the world's servers. Legacy confirmed. </legacy>
